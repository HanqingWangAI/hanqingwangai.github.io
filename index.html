About Me


Hi, I’m Hanqing Wang, an Embodied AI researcher at the Shanghai Artificial Intelligence Laboratory. My research interests focus on robot learning and embodied AI, exploring how intelligent systems can interact with and learn from their environments. I hold a Ph.D. from the Media Computing and Intelligent Systems Laboratory at the Beijing Institute of Technology, where I worked under the guidance of Prof. Wei Liang. During my Ph.D., I collaborated with Prof. Lap-Fai(Craig) Yu at George Mason University and held research internships at Microsoft Research Asia, mentored by Jiaolong Yang, and at the Inception Institute of Artificial Intelligence, where I worked with Prof. Jianbing Shen. I also spent time as a visiting student at ETH Zürich’s Computer Vision Lab, supervised by Prof. Luc Van Gool and Prof. Wenguan Wang.


I am currently looking for motivated interns interested in robot learning and embodied AI to join our team. If you're excited about developing cutting-edge research in intelligent systems, please feel free to reach out with your CV and a brief statement of your research interests.
